# How Generative AI Disrupts Search: An Empirical Study of Google Search, Gemini, and AI Overviews

This repository accompanies SIGIR 2026 paper "How Generative AI Disrupts Search: An Empirical Study of Google Search, Gemini, and AI Overviews paper." It contains the query dataset used to retrieve SERP, AIO, and Gemini outputs, along with scripts used to retrieve and analyze those outputs. 

## Repository structure
- `data/`: 
  - prompt templates for generating the synthetic query variants used in the benchmark.
    - `data/keywords_to_compare_qns_prompt.txt`: turns retail product keywords into comparison-style queries.
    - `data/keywords_to_qns_prompt.txt`: turns retail product keywords into natural-language product questions.
    - `data/qns_to_keywords_prompt.txt`: converts Natural Questions into keyword-form queries.
  - core CSV datasets used throughout collection and analysis.
    - `data/AIO_Benchmark_Dataset_v1.csv`: main 11,500-query benchmark with metadata (dataset label, query text, IDs, AIO flags, labels).
    - `data/AIO_Benchmark_Dataset_Robust_Changes.csv`: paired A/B cosmetic variants for robustness experiments on AI Overview and Gemini outputs.
    - `data/AIO_robustness_manual_data.csv`: manually annotated robustness subset for qualitative/quantitative checks.
    - `data/tranco_6GWVX.csv`: Tranco domain ranking snapshot used for source/domain prevalence analysis.
    - `data/SIGIR_regression.zip`: Contains .csv file with all domain-level characteristics to be able to replicate regression results in the   paper.
    - `data/benchmark_dataset_link.zip`: Contains a .csv with all retrieved links from the three search engines for Benchmark queries.
    - `data/politicians_links.csv`: Contains all retrieved links from the three search engines for Politician queries.
    - `data/trends_links.csv`: Contains all retrieved links from the three search engines for Google Trends queries.
    - `data/domains_for_categorization_1_2_26.csv`: Contains all unique domains from the Benchmark dataset, for which domain-level characteristics are collected.
- `scripts/`: data collection, crawling, and analysis code/notebooks.
  - `scripts/ai_chatbot_data_collection.py`: collects Gemini 2.5 Flash responses (optionally with Google Search grounding), attaches citations, tracks token usage and estimated cost, and streams results to JSONL for each query ID.
  - `scripts/production_crawler_debug.ipynb`: debugging notebook for SERP crawling; includes SerpAPI-based Google AI Overview/Bing fetch helpers, redirect resolution for Bing sources, and batch execution over benchmark IDs.
  - `scripts/Google_AIO_Benchmark_Analysis_edited_figure.ipynb`: analyzes AIO presence across dataset slices (e.g., retail vs. localized vs. ORCAS), question vs. non-question wording, query length, and location variants; produces figures for the paper.
  - `scripts/Popular_sources.ipynb`: compares Google AI Overview and Gemini references against traditional SERP sources, counts references per result, normalizes domains, and visualizes source/domain differences.
  - `scripts/ndcg_RBO_calculations.ipynb`: Computes similarity metrics of nDCG, RBO, and Jaccard Similarity for sources retrieved by Gemini, AIO, and SERP.
  -  `scripts/SIGIR_Regressions.ipynb`: Sets up dataset and runs regression results from the paper.
  - `scripts/gem_emb_diff.ipynb`: joins AI Overview (A/B) and Gemini (A/B) responses, computes embeddings with `gemini-embedding-001`, and reports cosine, Jaccard, length, and RBO correlations across model outputs.


## Benchmark query dataset
The benchmark contains 11,500 queries. We adapt and extend prior query collections used to study search and generative retrieval behavior, adding broader coverage of localized intent (e.g., "near X" queries) and syntactic form (keywords vs. full questions). The benchmark is partitioned into nine categories:
- ORCAS: real-user queries re-sampled and labeled as informational/transactional/navigational.
- Amazon Retail: real Amazon retail keyword queries.
- Amazon Retail Comp: product-comparison queries generated by Gemini 2.5 Flash based on Amazon Retail.
- Amazon Retail Q: natural-language product questions generated by Gemini 2.5 Flash based on Amazon Retail.
- Debate: debate-style queries.
- ELI5: complex questions sampled from a Reddit-based corpus.
- Localized: ORCAS "near me" queries instantiated with representative cities.
- NQ: sampled Natural Questions.
- NQ Keywords: keyword-style reformulations generated by Gemini 2.5 Flash from the Natural Questions set.


## Dataset creation procedure
Final dataset: 11,500 queries, based on the work of Puerto et al., 2025 and Aggarwal et al., 2024. We add queries to study localized queries and query syntactic structure (e.g., keywords vs. full statement/question).

- 500 retail product keyword queries: same queries from Puerto et al., 2025 (which are taken from a dataset of real Amazon queries) to address shortcomings of the Aggarwal et al. dataset.
- 500 retail product comparison queries: Gemini 2.5 Flash creates 500 realistic comparisons between the target products of the retail keyword subset and competitor(s) using the prompt in `data/keywords_to_compare_qns_prompt.txt`.
- 500 retail question queries: Gemini 2.5 Flash creates 500 realistic questions about the target products of the retail keyword subset using the prompt in `data/keywords_to_qns_prompt.txt`.
- 1,000 localized queries: randomly selected from the ORCAS dataset by searching for any query ending with "near me". Then 250 are kept untouched, and 250 each are randomly assigned to be replaced with "near Shanghai", "near Alicante, Spain", and "near Boulder, Colorado" to represent localized queries in a huge city (population >> 1 million), large city (population < 1 million), and medium-sized city (population ~100K) from various geographic locations.
- 1,000 debate queries: subsets of the davinci-Debate dataset used in Aggarwal and Puerto, but we take the full 1,000 queries from Liu et al., 2023.
- 1,000 complex question queries: from a Reddit dataset commonly used in query/QA literature including Aggarwal et al., 2024. We randomly sampled 1,000 queries.
- 1,000 Natural Question queries: real user questions to Google Search (all informational queries). Used in Aggarwal et al., 2024 and Puerto et al., 2025. We sample from the ones used in Aggarwal et al., 2024.
- 1,000 Natural Question keyword queries: transform the Natural Question queries from the subset above into a list of keywords that maintain the key ideas using the prompt in `data/qns_to_keywords_prompt.txt` with Gemini 2.5 Flash.
- 5,000 real user queries: sampled from the ORCAS dataset, these are real user queries of any kind (no filtering) to the Bing search engine. Used in Aggarwal et al., 2024, but we resample to get more queries. Queries are labeled by classifier into informational, transactional, or navigational.
